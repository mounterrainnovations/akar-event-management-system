## 4. Competitive Gap Analysis

### What Competitors Do Well ✅

- Flexible ticketing and registration
- Payment gateway integrations
- Basic CRM and attendee tracking
- Social media promotion tools
- Check-in and badge printing

### What Competitors Miss ❌

- **Integrated EdTech (LMS)** — No platform combines event management with course delivery
- **Indian Language Support** — Most platforms are English-only or have basic Hindi
- **Volunteer Management** — Usually a bolt-on, not core functionality
- **Impact Tracking Dashboard** — Few platforms show beneficiary impact metrics
- **Government Scheme Integration** — No competitor integrates with Indian govt schemes
- **Certificate Generation** — Post-workshop/training certificate automation is rare
- **Beneficiary Tracking** — Tracking the same beneficiary across multiple events/programs
- **Donor CRM with 80G receipts** — Automated tax receipt generation is uncommon
- **WhatsApp Integration** — Critical for India, mostly absent
- **Offline-first capability** — For events in areas with poor connectivity

## 6. Market Opportunity

### TAM (Total Addressable Market)

- **3.4 million+ registered NGOs** in India (NITI Aayog Darpan)
- **₹1.5 lakh crore** estimated annual CSR spend in India
- Growing demand for digital-first NGO operations post-COVID

### SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)

- **~50,000 women & child welfare NGOs** with active operations
- **~10,000 EdTech-focused NGOs** in India

### SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)

- **Initial target: 500-1,000 NGOs** in the women/child welfare & EdTech segment
- **Focus geographies:** Tier 1 & 2 cities in India

### Growth Drivers

1. **Digital India push** — Government incentivizing digital operations
2. **CSR mandates** — Companies need NGO partners with digital reporting
3. **Post-COVID digital adoption** — NGOs now expect online-first tools
4. **Donor expectations** — Donors expect transparency and digital engagement
5. **Youth volunteer base** — Young volunteers expect modern digital experiences
